Tribal Welfare Department


The Tribal Welfare Department, known as "ஊர் பணிமுகம் துறை" in Tamil, plays a vital role in advancing the welfare of indigenous communities. Tasked with implementing policies and programs, it aims to uplift the socio-economic status of tribal populations. Through initiatives in education, healthcare, and livelihood development, it strives to address the unique challenges faced by tribal groups. Additionally, the department works towards preserving and promoting the rich cultural heritage of indigenous communities while advocating for their rights and empowerment. Overall, it serves as a key facilitator in ensuring the holistic development and well-being of tribal societies.

Heritage Sites in Tamilnadu


Brihadeeswarar Temple, Thanjavur: Also known as the Big Temple, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a marvel of Dravidian architecture. Built by the Chola emperor Raja Raja I in the 11th century, it features a towering vimana (temple tower) and intricate sculptures depicting Hindu mythology.

Airavatesvara Temple, Darasuram: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, this temple is renowned for its architectural elegance and intricate carvings. Built by the Chola king Rajaraja II in the 12th century, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is known for its unique vimana shaped like a chariot.

Group of Monuments at Mahabalipuram: This UNESCO World Heritage Site comprises several ancient rock-cut temples and sculptures dating back to the Pallava dynasty (7th-9th centuries). Notable landmarks include the Shore Temple, Arjuna's Penance, and the Five Rathas, showcasing exquisite craftsmanship.

Chola Temples at Gangaikonda Cholapuram and Darasuram: These temples, built by the Chola dynasty in the 11th and 12th centuries respectively, are UNESCO World Heritage Sites. They exemplify the architectural grandeur and religious fervor of the Chola period, with intricate carvings and towering vimanas.

Madurai Meenakshi Amman Temple: One of the largest and most famous temples in India, the Meenakshi Temple is dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (an incarnation of Parvati) and Lord Sundareswarar (Shiva). It is known for its elaborate gopurams (gateway towers) adorned with colorful sculptures.

Thanjavur Maratha Palace: Also known as the Thanjavur Palace, this historic complex was built by the Nayak and Maratha rulers of Thanjavur. It houses the Saraswathi Mahal Library, which preserves a vast collection of ancient manuscripts and artworks.
